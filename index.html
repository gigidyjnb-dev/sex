Marcus's biggest problem: he can't find his treatment completion certificate from 2013. The platform tells him exactly how to get it — and what to do if the program has closed. He also needs his probation completion letter. Here's how he gets both.

HOW TO GET YOUR RECORDS

Treatment Certificate — Program May Be Closed

Option A — Program Still Open
Call or write to the treatment provider directly. Ask for a Certificate of Completion or an official letter on letterhead confirming your dates of participation and successful completion. Use the Document Drafter to generate the request letter in 60 seconds.
Option B — Program Closed
Contact the Ohio Department of Mental Health and Addiction Services (OhioMHAS) at 1-614-466-2596. They license treatment programs and keep records of closed providers. Ask them who acquired the records when the program closed.
Option C — Court Ordered It
Request your court file from the clerk. The sentencing order will show the treatment was ordered and your probation termination order will confirm it was completed. This can substitute for the certificate in many cases.
Probation Completion Letter
Write to the probation department of the court that supervised you. Use the Document Drafter → "Probation Completion Request." Print it, sign it, mail it certified. They are legally required to respond. Turnaround is usually 2–4 weeks.

✅ What Happens After You File The clerk stamps your petition and gives you a case number. The court schedules a hearing — usually 30–90 days out. The prosecutor may object or may not appear. You attend, present your documents, speak briefly about your life and rehabilitation, and the judge rules. If approved, you receive a court order removing you from the registry. You then deliver certified copies of that order to your county sheriff and to the Ohio SORN (Sex Offender Registration and Notification) office. Within 30 days, your name is removed.
